UX/UI design for a management interface dedicated to contract monitoring and work progress (SAL). A complex ecosystem transforming raw operational data into precise, transparent billing for major service consortiums.

View in Timeline

1. Communication & Collaboration

Aligning cross-departmental business logic

The Challenge: As an internal platform for CNS (Consorzio Nazionale Servizi), the primary hurdle was aligning the needs of diverse departments: from administrative teams managing contracts to operators monitoring daily workflows.

Iterative Workshops: I led collaborative sessions on Figma to map the entire contract lifecycle.

Consistent Design System: I strictly followed client brand guidelines for typography and color palettes, ensuring the tool felt like a native, integrated part of the corporate ecosystem.

2. Process & User Focus

Reducing human error in sensitive data environments

Enterprise users require control and speed. The interface was structured to minimize cognitive load during the entry of sensitive accounting data.

Core Operational Features

  • Critical Control Dashboard: A home screen designed to highlight immediate issues, such as "out-of-package" contracts or upcoming deadlines, using high-contrast numerical cards.
  • Modular Data Entry: The contract creation flow is divided into logical blocks (Details, Lots, Additional Packages), helping operators maintain focus during long, complex form sessions.
  • Contextual Navigation: Clear visual markers allow users to switch between different contract phases without losing their progress.

3. Problem-Solving & Creativity

From raw data to billing transparency

The Problem: Justifying extra costs to the final client was a major pain point. Data regarding call minutes and inbound/outbound contact volumes were often difficult to interpret and prone to disputes.

The Visual Solution: I introduced automated annual and monthly summary tables that calculate the "Delta" (variance) between contracted volumes and actual service usage. This transformation from raw tables to a "Billing Authorization Request" is now immediate and evidence-based.

4. Execution & Best Practices

Scalability through Atomic Design

  • Figma High-Fidelity: Mockups were built using atomic components, allowing for the management of dozens of states (editing, read-only, validation errors) while maintaining pixel-perfect consistency.
  • Semantic UX: Strategic use of color (e.g., Red for "Out-of-Package" alerts) guides the user's attention to financial discrepancies instantly.
