Industrial SaaS Solution

Production

UX/UI design and front-end implementation for a manufacturing interface dedicated to the production of Laser/LiDAR sensors. A solution designed to replace legacy Excel files, perfectly integrated with the Odoo ERP system.

View in Timeline

1. Communication & Collaboration

Aligning cross-functional teams and technical constraints

The Challenge: The initial request was to replace Excel spreadsheets, but early research revealed a deeper issue: the misalignment between digital tools and real production workflows. I reframed the problem from a tooling issue to a system and cognitive load problem, focusing on how operators process information under pressure.

Cross-functional Alignment: I facilitated workshops with production managers, operators, and engineers to align on goals, constraints, and mental models. This helped create a shared understanding between technical and non-technical stakeholders, ensuring that design decisions balanced user needs, business priorities, and system limitations.

Continuous Feedback Loop: I established an iterative design process with weekly reviews, translating complex system behaviors into intuitive interactions. This allowed stakeholders to validate decisions early and ensured the product evolved based on real user feedback, not assumptions.

2. Process & User Focus

Designing for the physical factory environment

The system was designed for a high-pressure industrial environment, where operators interact with tools under time constraints and physical limitations.

Core Methodological Steps

  • User Research: Through on-site observation and shadowing, I identified that Excel-based workflows caused cognitive overload due to fragmented information, lack of feedback, and poor traceability.
  • Problem Framing:I defined the core design challenge as reducing mental effort and preventing errors, rather than simply improving usability.
  • Contextual Design: I designed interfaces optimized for real-world conditions (limited attention, repetitive tasks), using large interaction targets, clear hierarchy, and high legibility.
  • Iterative Prototyping: I moved quickly from paper prototypes to interactive flows, validating decisions directly with operators to ensure the system matched their mental models and workflows.

3. Problem-Solving & Creativity

Designing systems, not just interfaces

The Problem: The main technical challenge was integrating the new system with Odoo ERP without disrupting production workflows or introducing additional complexity.

The Visual Solution: We developed custom Odoo modules. From a UX perspective, I designed an invisible experience where users synchronize data without perceiving the platform jump. Furthermore, I implemented a strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) system. Each workstation only displays the tools relevant to the logged-in operator, drastically simplifying the UI and ensuring data security. Error-prevention mechanics, like visual alerts for obsolete BOM revisions, were integrated to prevent fatal assembly errors.

4. Execution & Best Practices

End-to-end delivery and measurable business impact

  • Front-end Implementation: I went beyond design deliverables, actively coding the front-end and back-end implementation to guarantee pixel-perfect fidelity and ensure that interactive behaviors matched the initial vision.
  • Scalable UI System: Built a comprehensive library of reusable UI components (data tables, functional buttons, search filters) to maintain visual consistency and accelerate future module development.
  • Measurable Results: Achieved a 45% reduction in human error during data entry, decreased new operator onboarding time by 30% due to simplified, role-specific UIs, and saved the management team 12 hours a week through automated Odoo synchronization.
